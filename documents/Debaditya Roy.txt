Debaditya Roy is an Assistant Professor at the Department of CSE, Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.

Debaditya Roy was a Senior Scientist at the Institute of High-Performance Computing, A*STAR, Singapore working on Action Anticipation, Situated Reasoning and Neurosymbolic AI with Dr. Basura Fernando. 

Previously, Debaditya Roy was a post-doctoral researcher working with Prof. Atsushi Fukuda in the Department of Transportation Engineering  at Nihon University, Japan in the M2Smart project funded by JICA and JST. 

Debaditya Roy received his doctoral degree from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad as a member of the Visual Learning and Intelligence (VIGIL) lab. Debaditya Roy was advised by Prof. C. Krishna Mohan and my thesis was titled Representation Learning for Action Recognition. Debaditya Roy's work on snatch theft detection was featured in The Hindu, a leading daily newspaper in India.
